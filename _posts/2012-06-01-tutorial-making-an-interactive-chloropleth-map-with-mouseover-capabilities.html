In my last post, I explored how open-source cartographic tools and packages like CartoDB and Leaflet can make interactive maps sexier and more robust than the average Google Fusions table. But I wasn't able to figure out a good way to add mouseover/hover capabilities to my polygon  (see The New York World's recent Stop and Frisk Hotspots map for a good example). I came close while using GeoJSON data to render the polygons. But I couldn't easily assign an integer value to all 159 GeoJSON polygons, and doing it by hand would have taken hours.

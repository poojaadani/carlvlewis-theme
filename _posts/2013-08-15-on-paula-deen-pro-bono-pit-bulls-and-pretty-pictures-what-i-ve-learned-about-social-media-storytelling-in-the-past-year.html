Two days from now will mark exactly one year since I accepted a position here in Savannah, Ga. on an unforeseen whim prompted by a call from our  V.P. of Audience and digital media visionary Steve Yelvington. According to my job description, I head up digital content strategy and news product development efforts for the soon-to-be rebranded "Savannah Media Network" (known by most folks simply by the acronym SMN, which conveniently also stands for Savannah Morning News – our daily print newspaper product  – but consists of much, much more).  As you can tell from my  precipitous decline in blogging frequency, I've been tasked with a big workload – one that varies day-by-day from programming news apps, to formatting content in Drupal, to good old-fashioned reporting.
